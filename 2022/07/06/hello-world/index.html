Playing with Light and Shadow in Street Photography

In street photography, usually the time in a day is not crucial as in other genres like landscape photography. Street photography is formless. No one’s gonna tell you where is the vantage point. No one’s gonna tell you when is the golden hour, when is the blue hour, which you are bound to take better photos. Not gonna happen. You define your own vantage point and best time. You can simply focus on capturing what happens around you. On this regard, street photography is the freest genre among all kinds of photography.

However, like in any other photography genre, light and shadow are your great friend and subject. While you can shoot whatever you want, if you pay heed to the light according to the time of the day, you can add a whole new dimension to your street photography.

Street Photography in “Golden Hour”

It’s very rare to hear the term “golden hour” in street photography because it’s not paramount here. If you happen to think it’s very important, you are probably not doing street photography but urban/cityscape photography. People tend to conflate the two when they are too lazy to check the definitions in Wikipedia. However, there are some proporties of golden hour lights that you can bear in mind, so that you leverage them to make better photos.
In the golden hour, which is usually an one hour time window after the dawn or before the dusk. At that time, the angle between the sunlight and the earth is acute. So the shadow can be quite long. The long shadow can be leveraged to be the leading line in your composition.
